# VISUAL STYLE — Beyond Work deck (paste your content brief at the bottom)

## What this is
A visual style guide for Beyond Work slides. Use this for every slide unless overridden. Slide content goes at the bottom under
"CONTENT" — this section only describes how slides should look, not what they should say.

## Tone
Calm, intelligent, inevitable. Editorial, not marketing. Reference set: Stripe, Linear, Datadog, Anthropic. Never consumer-app, never
hype-reel.

## Format
- 16:9 aspect.
- Two slide modes that alternate:
  1. **Act-opener slides** — full-bleed dark cinematic nature photograph (rock, canyon, terrain, forest, geological textures). Used
between sections.
  2. **Content slides** — warm cream background. The default mode.

## Palette
- Cream background: `#F5F1E8`
- Deep forest green (body type, lines, charts): `#1A3D2E`
- Mint accent (italic emphasis, accent bars, highlight in charts): `#6FE3A8`
- Near-black for secondary text
- White for cream-on-dark situations

## Typography
- **Headlines:** editorial serif (Tiempos / GT Sectra family). Large, with tight letter-spacing. Italicise the emphasis word or phrase
and set it in mint — every headline should follow this rhythm: regular text + *italic mint accent.*
- **Eyebrows, footers, data labels, section markers:** monospace (Berkeley Mono family). ALL CAPS. Letter-spaced. Always mint green or
low-contrast forest. Always small.
- **Body:** clean serif or sans at reading size. Forest green or near-black.
- **Numerals:** monospace for section numbers (01, 02, 03, 04). Editorial serif italic for large hero numbers in charts/data.

## Slide anatomy — content mode
- **Top-left:** Beyond Work wordmark, small.
- **Top-right:** section label in monospace, small, mint (e.g. `HOW IT WORKS`).
- **Eyebrow:** a single short mint monospace label above each headline, preceded by a short dash ornament (`— LABEL`).
- **Headline:** editorial serif, large, italic mint on the emphasis word.
- **Body:** generous whitespace beneath.
- **Numbered sections** (when listing): `01` `02` `03` `04` in monospace, paired with serif labels.
- **Thin horizontal rules** in forest green to separate sections.
- **Footer:** monospace metadata strip across the bottom, low contrast (e.g. `BEYOND WORK · CONFIDENTIAL · MAY 2026`).

## Slide anatomy — act-opener mode
- Full-bleed dark moody nature photograph (terrain, canyon, rock face, forest — never people, never office, never abstract).
- Small wordmark top-left, small section indicator top-right (`ACT II`, `NO. II`).
- Monospace eyebrow lower-left in mint (`NO. II · HOW IT WORKS`).
- Title bottom-left, very large editorial serif, two words on two lines: first word in cream, second word in italic mint. Pattern:
*The* | *mechanism.* / *The* | *edge.* / *The* | *proof.*
- Thin horizontal rule across the bottom.

## Diagrams and charts
- Tufte-simple. No 3D, no gradients, no decoration.
- Forest green and mint as the only colours.
- Bar charts: forest fill, mint accent for the highlighted bar.
- Architecture diagrams: thin forest outlines, mint highlights for emphasis, monospace labels.
- Italic serif for chart numbers in mint when they're the hero.

## Layout principles
- One idea per slide. If a slide needs two ideas, split it.
- Generous whitespace. Editorial margins (wider than typical decks).
- Left-aligned for editorial slides, never centered unless it's a hero number or a cinematic title.
- Numbered grids (3-up, 4-up) are fine for comparison slides — use a thin vertical rule between columns and a monospace label above
each.
- Sources, citations, figure references in monospace, low contrast, never in body weight.

## What to leave out
- No icons unless load-bearing.
- No drop shadows, gradients, glows, or sparkles.
- No "AI" visual clichés (brains, circuits, neural nets, robots).
- No stock illustration. No corporate photography.
- No exclamation marks except for genuine celebration.
- No people on slides unless they're named team / customer.
